The first party-list elections were held in 1998. With overwhelming public support, Joseph
Estrada was elected President of the Republic. In January 2001, EDSA 2, a follow-up to
People Power in 1986, ousted Estrada from Malacaang and installed Gloria Macapagal-Arroyo
as the next president. Senatorial and local elections were conducted four months later,
resulting in a decisive success for the new administration when its allies won a majority in
both Houses of Congress. Along with gaining the majority of the elected local positions,
Congress was elected. President Arroyo, on the other hand, had become so unpopular that
she just narrowly defeated her closest competitor, actor-turned-politician Fernando Poe,
Jr., in the 2004 election. President Arroyo was challenged with the "Hello, Garci" incident
a year later, in June 2005, in which she was accused of taping conversations with COMELEC
Commissioner Garciliano with the goal of rigging the elections. The information was leaked
to the media.

While President Arroyo managed to stay in power, she was unable to win over the majority
of Filipinos, culminating in a landslide victory for the opposition in the 2007 senatorial
elections.
