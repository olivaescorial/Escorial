In 1978, he called for the election of an Interim Batasang Pambansa due to popular pressure.
The Kilusang Bagong Lipunan (KBL) was elected as the government's party. The election was
billed as restoring Philippine democracy, but Marcos' goal was to garner legitimacy for his
authoritarian government and establish a rubber-stamp legislature. The election defied
democratic procedures and was marked by the Marcos government's widespread use of electoral
manipulation to achieve victory. In 1980, local elections were marred by rampant terrorism,
bloodshed, and massive fraud. Both urban and rural residents were vocal in their criticism.
Marcos resigned himself to the election process in order to reclaim his legitimacy. The political
opposition boycotted the ceremony, instead launching a broad-based anti-dictatorship movement.

The time was marked by protests against the rule of the late President Benigno Aquino. Elections
for the Batasang Pambansa in 1984 were boycotted by the political opposition. The exercise, which
was once again boycotted by the political opposition, was meant to shift public attention away from
the killing of Senator Benigno Aquino. Nonetheless, the time was marked by Marcos' waning popularity
and the people's developing political opposition to his tyranny.
