In 1986, Marcos called for quick presidential elections in response to internal and international
pressure. In the same year, the EDSA rebellion was sparked by extensive election rigging and
irregularities, as well as a deteriorating social, political, and economic order. The People Power
Revolution of 1986 resulted in the deposition of Marcos, the demise of KBL, and the election of
Corazon Aquino as President.

Three major electoral exercises were used as part of Aquino's program to restore democracy,
promote stability, and establish political legitimacy: A multi-party system was established by the
1987 Constitution. People voted for a president in synchronized national and local elections in 1992.
The elimination of "trapo", or traditional politics, was a major campaign theme. In 1995, new type
of fraud known as "dagdag-bawas" was used or the subtraction of votes from one candidate to be
added to the opposing candidate, was used.
